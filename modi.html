About the Prime Minister

Narendra Modi is the 14th and current Prime Minister of India, serving continuously since May 2014. Born into a modest family in Vadnagar, Gujarat, his journey from a humble tea-seller to the highest executive office in the world's most populous democracy stands as one of the most remarkable political stories of the 21st century.

Modi began his political career through the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) and joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in 1987. He rose to become the Chief Minister of Gujarat in 2001, a position he held for over 13 years — the longest-serving CM of the state.

Under his leadership, India has witnessed transformative policies including the Digital India mission, Make in India, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, and the landmark Goods and Services Tax (GST) reform. He is known for his strong foreign policy stance and has positioned India as a leading voice on the global stage.

In 2024, Modi was elected to a third consecutive term as Prime Minister, a feat achieved by very few leaders in independent India's history, reinforcing his dominant standing in Indian politics.

Career
Political Journey
1971
RSS Full-Time Worker (Pracharak)
Devoted himself to the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh as a full-time organiser, travelling across India and building grassroots connections.
1987
Joins Bharatiya Janata Party
Joined the BJP and quickly rose through party ranks due to his organisational skills and mass connect with common people.
2001
Chief Minister of Gujarat
Appointed Chief Minister of Gujarat, beginning a transformative 13-year tenure focused on economic development, infrastructure, and governance reforms.
2002 – 2014
Gujarat Model of Development
Led Gujarat to become one of India's fastest-growing states. Spearheaded Vibrant Gujarat summits attracting billions in investment, and transformed infrastructure.
May 2014
Prime Minister of India — 1st Term
Led the BJP to a historic majority win in the 2014 general elections — the first outright majority by a single party since 1984 — and was sworn in as the 14th Prime Minister of India.
May 2019
Prime Minister — 2nd Term
BJP won an even larger majority in the 2019 elections. Modi continued landmark reforms including abrogation of Article 370 in Jammu & Kashmir and passage of the Citizenship Amendment Act.
June 2024
Prime Minister — Historic 3rd Term
Sworn in for a third consecutive term, Modi became only the second Prime Minister after Jawaharlal Nehru to achieve this, further cementing his legacy in India's democratic history.
Governance
Key Initiatives & Programmes
🏗️
Make in India
Launched in 2014 to transform India into a global manufacturing hub, attracting FDI across 25+ sectors and boosting domestic production.
📱
Digital India
Expanded internet connectivity to rural India, launched UPI payments, and pushed India to become one of the world's largest digital economies.
🧹
Swachh Bharat Abhiyan
Clean India mission resulted in construction of over 100 million toilets, dramatically reducing open defecation and improving public health nationwide.
🏠
Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana
Housing for All scheme under which over 30 million affordable homes have been built for families in rural and urban India.
💡
Ujjwala Yojana
Provided over 90 million free LPG connections to women from below-poverty-line families, transforming household energy access across rural India.
🏥
Ayushman Bharat
World's largest government-funded health insurance scheme providing health coverage of ₹5 lakh per family per year to over 500 million beneficiaries.
🛤️
Infrastructure Push
Record highway construction, expansion of railways, new metro networks across 20+ cities, and launch of Vande Bharat Express trains.
☀️
International Solar Alliance
Co-founded the ISA at COP21 with France to promote solar energy globally and accelerate India's renewable energy targets.
💰
GST Reform
Implemented the Goods and Services Tax in 2017, unifying India's complex multi-layered tax structure into "One Nation, One Tax."
